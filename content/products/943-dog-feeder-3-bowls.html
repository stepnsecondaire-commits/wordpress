Three-bowl automatic dog feeder for large breeds and multi-dog households

This product solves a specific problem in the pet feeder category: large dogs and multi-dog households need larger portions, stronger construction, and multiple dispensing points. Standard single-bowl feeders designed for cats and small dogs fail under the daily load of a Golden Retriever or a Labrador. This model is engineered specifically for that use case.

Three separate bowls allow scheduled feeding across multiple dogs or multiple meals with clear separation. The 6-liter hopper capacity holds 5 to 7 days of food for a medium-to-large dog. Tuya Smart 2.4G WiFi is standard, giving brands a fast path to a white-label app.

Multi-bowl architecture for multi-dog households

The three-bowl design addresses a niche but high-value buyer segment: households with two or three dogs who compete at meal time. Instead of purchasing three separate feeders, the owner uses one unit with three scheduled dispense points. This works for:

  • Two or three dogs of similar size eating similar portions at the same time
  • One dog with three meals per day spread across the bowls (useful for dogs with gastric issues who need smaller, more frequent meals)
  • Medical diet separation when one dog needs a prescription and others do not (by using different bowls for different food types, though this requires manual hopper changes)

Construction for large-dog durability

Large dogs destroy cheap pet feeders. They push, knock, chew, and lean on the unit. This model is built for that:

  • ABS + PC + 304 stainless steel construction: the main body is ABS + polycarbonate for impact resistance, and the internal parts that contact food use 304 stainless steel for hygiene and durability. 304 stainless is the same grade used in commercial kitchen equipment.
  • 6-liter hopper: sized for multi-day feeding of large dogs (200 to 350 grams per meal typical for 15 to 30 kg breeds)
  • Weighted base: the unit is bottom-heavy to prevent large dogs from tipping it
  • Robust hopper lid: designed so a determined Labrador cannot open it even with persistent attempts

Target buyer segments

The core buyer segments for this product are:

  • Large breed dog owners: Golden Retrievers, Labradors, German Shepherds, Huskies, Mastiffs. These breeds eat enough daily food that single-meal dispensing is a real convenience.
  • Multi-dog households: 2 or 3 dogs sharing a household, often the reason for buying an automatic feeder in the first place.
  • Working dog handlers: hunters, farmers, service dog handlers who need predictable feeding for multiple dogs while they are away.
  • Dog boarding facilities: small-scale boarding businesses that can use the 3-bowl design to feed 3 dogs from a single automated station.

The multi-dog household segment is the single strongest marketing angle. Product content and ads should emphasize "feed 2 or 3 dogs automatically from one unit".

Color options and brand positioning

Standard colors: black and white. Black is the most common choice for masculine brand positioning (fits premium home decor, appeals to male dog owners). White is the secondary choice for brands targeting modern home aesthetics. Custom colors available at 500+ unit orders.

Certifications

Standard package: CE + RED + EMC for EU (Tuya WiFi module is pre-certified), FCC for US, ROHS, REACH with SVHC screening, FDA 21 CFR 177.1520 for the PP/ABS food-contact components, and specific food-contact testing for the 304 stainless steel parts under EU 10/2011 and FDA 21 CFR 175.300.

UKCA for Great Britain and PSE for Japan are available on request at additional test fees.

Container loadability

Unit dimensions 44 x 35 x 35 cm. Net weight approximately 2.0 kg. Carton 42 x 42 x 53 cm holds 8 units (16 kg gross per carton). A 40-foot high-cube container holds approximately 450 to 550 units. This is a strong volumetric efficiency for a multi-bowl dog feeder, which translates to USD 3 to 5 per unit in ocean freight landed cost.
