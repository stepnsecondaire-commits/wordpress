Smart Pet Feeders: The Complete Wholesale Buyer's Guide

Smart pet feeders are the second-largest category in smart pet products by unit volume, behind automatic cat litter boxes, and they are the first automated pet product that most pet owners buy. Global unit sales for WiFi-connected pet feeders crossed 8 million in 2024, and the category now accounts for roughly 18% of total smart pet products revenue worldwide. For B2B buyers (importers, distributors, private-label brands, e-commerce operators), smart pet feeders are attractive because of three reasons: they are technically simpler than cat litter boxes so unit costs are lower, the retail-to-FOB margin multiple is comparable, and they sell across the full spectrum of pet owners (single cat, multi-pet, dog owners of every breed size). This guide is the full B2B framework for sourcing smart pet feeders from China, written from the factory side by Eviehome (Hefei Ecologie Vie Home Technology Co., Ltd.) based in Hefei, China.

The four families of smart pet feeders

Dry food dispensers

The most common category. A hopper holds 3 to 6 liters of kibble, an auger or impeller dispenses the portion into the bowl at the scheduled time. Battery, mains, or both. WiFi optional but now standard in the mid-range and above. Target market: cat and small dog owners, portion control for weight management. FOB cost (500 units): USD 18 to USD 45 depending on capacity, WiFi, camera option. Retail USD 79 to USD 249.

Smart feeders with camera

Same dry food dispensing mechanism with a built-in HD camera (usually 1080p, sometimes 2K) mounted above or inside the unit. The camera streams to the owner's phone via the app, plays audio back to the pet, and sometimes records a short clip of every feeding. Premium segment that appeals to guilt-prone pet owners who travel for work. FOB cost: USD 35 to USD 75. Retail USD 149 to USD 399.

Wet food feeders

Sealed compartments that open on schedule, keeping wet food fresh for up to 12 hours. Some models add ice packs or Peltier cooling. Niche segment but growing with the shift toward wet and fresh food in the US and EU markets. FOB cost: USD 40 to USD 90. Retail USD 149 to USD 299.

Large pet feeders

Higher capacity units (8 to 15 liters) for medium and large dog owners, multi-dog households, and commercial use (kennels, shelters). Reinforced construction, heavier bases to prevent pet tipping, dual bowls for two pets sharing. FOB cost: USD 35 to USD 80. Retail USD 149 to USD 349.

Specifications that matter when you evaluate a smart feeder for wholesale

  1. Hopper capacity. 3 liters (single cat, 7 to 10 days of food), 4 to 5 liters (standard), 6 liters (large dogs or multi-pet).
  2. Portion accuracy. Quality units dispense within +/- 2 grams of the programmed portion. Cheap units have +/- 10 grams variance, which defeats the purpose of portion control.
  3. Dispenser mechanism. Auger screw (most common, reliable), paddle wheel (cheaper, more jams), rotating disc (premium, best for odd-shaped kibble). Confirm compatibility with your target market's kibble size (American kibble averages 8 to 12 mm, European sometimes smaller).
  4. Anti-jam protection. Essential. Premium units reverse the motor automatically on detection of a blockage. Cheap units simply stop, leaving the pet without food for a full meal cycle.
  5. Power. Dual power (AC adapter + battery backup) is the B2B standard in 2026. Battery-only units are for secondary markets, AC-only units are for rental properties. Always confirm battery runtime (minimum 30 days on 4 D-cell batteries).
  6. WiFi and app. 2.4 GHz WiFi, Bluetooth pairing, iOS and Android app. Tuya white-label app is the fastest OEM path. Native app development doubles the cost but lets you control the brand experience and data.
  7. Scheduling flexibility. Minimum 4 meals per day, ideally 10 or more. Portion size adjustable from 5 grams to 150 grams in 5-gram increments.
  8. Camera (if applicable). 1080p is the entry level, 2K is standard, 4K is premium. Night vision required. Two-way audio standard.
  9. Food safety. All parts in contact with food must be food-grade ABS or stainless steel 304, certified FDA for the US market and EU 10/2011 for Europe.
  10. Noise level during dispensing. Under 45 dB. Cats are scared by loud dispensers and stop eating from them.

Frequently asked questions

What is the MOQ for a private-label smart pet feeder?

Standard MOQ is 500 units per SKU for wholesale and ODM with cosmetic customization. 300 units is sometimes negotiable for first-time customers on standard models. OEM with new tooling starts at 1 000 units.

How long does first-order manufacturing take for smart pet feeders?

30 to 45 days from deposit payment to shipped container for wholesale orders, 45 to 70 days for ODM with custom firmware, 75 to 100 days for OEM with new tooling.

What certifications are required for pet feeders sold in the United States and Europe?

FCC Part 15 for the WiFi module (US), CE EMC Directive 2014/30/EU (EU), ROHS, REACH, and food-contact compliance (FDA 21 CFR for the US, Regulation EU 10/2011 for Europe) for any part in contact with kibble or water. UKCA marking is required for the UK post-Brexit. See our full certifications page.

Do smart pet feeders work during a WiFi outage?

Quality units store the scheduled meal plan locally on the controller and continue to dispense even if WiFi drops. Only the app control, camera streaming and remote notifications are offline during the outage. Always verify this on the sample before committing to an order: cheap units depend on cloud connection for every dispense, which is unacceptable for a feeding device.
