For a B2B buyer deciding which products to source, the Amazon best seller lists are the single most valuable real-time signal of what is selling now. This article analyzes the Amazon best seller categories for smart pet products in early 2026, explains what the rankings mean, and extracts actionable insights for new brand entrants. How Amazon best seller rankings work

Amazon publishes a Best Sellers Rank (BSR) for every product in every category and sub-category. BSR is updated hourly and reflects recent sales velocity, not total sales. A lower BSR means higher recent sales.

For category analysis:

  • Category BSR below 100: top seller, strong demand, crowded field
  • Category BSR 100 to 500: solid seller, meaningful market
  • Category BSR 500 to 5 000: niche but viable, lower competition
  • Category BSR above 5 000: slow seller, risky category

BSR is relative to the category size. A BSR of 200 in "Pet Supplies" is much better than a BSR of 200 in "Electronics".

Reading reviews for insight

Best seller lists tell you what sells. Reviews tell you why products fail. For any category you are considering, read:

  • The top 50 five-star reviews (what buyers love)
  • The top 100 one-star reviews (what buyers hate)
  • The top 30 three-star reviews (honest trade-offs)

Patterns emerge quickly. In automatic litter boxes, the #1 complaint is often "sensor failure" or "motor noise". In pet feeders, "app pairing" and "portion accuracy". In water fountains, "pump noise" and "filter availability". These are your improvement opportunities.

The BSR trap

Chasing BSR is dangerous. A low BSR (high sales) in a category can mean:

  • Healthy category, room for new entrants (good)
  • Crowded category with dominant incumbents (bad)
  • Category dominated by budget products with thin margins (bad)
  • Category with heavy ad spend required to compete (challenging)

BSR alone does not tell you which of these. Combine BSR with:

  • Number of competing listings
  • Average review count (high = mature market)
  • Average price (low = commoditized)
  • Ad cost (use Helium 10 or Jungle Scout for estimates)
  • New product launches per month

Frequently asked questions

Can a new brand break into a crowded best-seller category?

Yes, with strong differentiation. Crowded categories have proven demand, which is valuable. The entry tactic: identify a specific under-served sub-segment (multi-cat households, long-haired breeds, senior pets) and position aggressively to that segment.

Should I source what is already selling or try something new?

For a first product, source what is selling. Validate demand before investing in category creation. Once you have operational muscle, experiment with new categories.
