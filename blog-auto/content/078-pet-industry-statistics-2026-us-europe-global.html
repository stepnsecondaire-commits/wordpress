Pet Industry Statistics 2026: US, Europe and Global Overview

Numbers matter in B2B. When pitching pet products to retailers, distributors or investors, a concrete statistic carries more weight than a vague trend. This article compiles the most useful pet industry statistics for 2026, sourced from APPA (American Pet Products Association), FEDIAF (European Pet Food Industry), Euromonitor, Statista and industry reports. Use these numbers to anchor your pitches, your market research, and your procurement decisions. Written from Hefei, China, by Eviehome (Hefei Ecologie Vie Home Technology Co., Ltd.).

Global pet industry size

  • Global pet industry 2024: USD 320 billion
  • Projected 2026: USD 350 to 370 billion
  • CAGR 2024 to 2028: 5 to 7 percent
  • Largest category: pet food (60 to 65 percent of total spend)
  • Fastest-growing category: smart pet products (+18 percent CAGR)

United States

Market size

  • US pet industry 2024: USD 147 billion (APPA)
  • Projected 2026: USD 160 to 170 billion
  • Pet food spend: USD 65 billion
  • Veterinary care: USD 38 billion
  • Supplies and over-the-counter medicine: USD 33 billion
  • Pet services: USD 11 billion

Pet ownership

  • US households with pets: 66 percent (86 million households)
  • Cat owners: 46.5 million households
  • Dog owners: 65 million households
  • Average cats per cat-owning household: 1.8
  • Average dogs per dog-owning household: 1.5
  • Pets as "family members": 85 percent of owners agree

Spending per pet

  • Average annual spend per cat: USD 1 150
  • Average annual spend per dog: USD 1 680
  • Millennial and Gen Z owners spend 15 to 30 percent more per pet than older generations

Europe

Market size

  • EU pet industry 2024: EUR 52 billion (FEDIAF)
  • Pet food spend EU: EUR 28 billion
  • Pet accessories and services: EUR 24 billion
  • Projected EU 2026: EUR 58 to 62 billion

Pet ownership in Europe

  • EU households with at least one pet: 50 percent (over 90 million households)
  • Total cats in EU: 127 million
  • Total dogs in EU: 106 million
  • Country with highest cat population: Germany (15.2 million)
  • Country with highest dog population: Germany (10.6 million)
  • Country with highest cat ownership percentage: Romania (43 percent of households)

Asia-Pacific

  • China pet industry 2024: RMB 290 billion (roughly USD 40 billion)
  • Japan pet industry 2024: JPY 1.6 trillion (roughly USD 11 billion)
  • South Korea pet industry 2024: KRW 6 trillion (roughly USD 4.5 billion)
  • Australia pet industry 2024: AUD 30 billion (roughly USD 20 billion)

Pet ownership Asia-Pacific

  • China households with pets: 17 percent (growing fast)
  • Japan households with pets: 23 percent
  • South Korea households with pets: 30 percent
  • Australia households with pets: 62 percent

Smart pet products specifically

  • Global smart pet products market 2024: USD 8.2 billion
  • Projected 2026: USD 11.5 billion
  • Projected 2028: USD 16 billion
  • CAGR: 18 percent (vs 5 to 7 percent for overall pet industry)
  • Largest sub-category: smart pet feeders (USD 1.6 billion 2024)
  • Fastest growing sub-category: smart bird feeders with camera (+40 percent YoY from small base)

Smart pet product penetration

  • US households with at least one smart pet product: 18 percent (growing from 8 percent in 2019)
  • EU households with at least one smart pet product: 12 percent
  • China households with at least one smart pet product: 25 percent (highest penetration globally)

E-commerce share of pet product sales

  • US 2024: 38 percent of all pet product sales online
  • US 2026 projected: 42 percent
  • EU 2024: 28 percent online
  • China 2024: 65 percent online (Taobao, JD, Tmall dominant)
  • Fastest growing channel: DTC brand websites (+25 percent YoY)

Channel shares (US 2024)

  • Amazon: 22 percent of all US pet product sales
  • Chewy: 12 percent
  • Walmart (physical and online): 14 percent
  • PetSmart: 11 percent
  • Petco: 7 percent
  • Specialty and independent retailers: 15 percent
  • DTC brand websites: 9 percent
  • Other (Target, grocery, etc.): 10 percent

Demographics of pet owners

US

  • Gen Z pet owners: 24 percent of US pet-owning households
  • Millennial pet owners: 33 percent
  • Gen X pet owners: 24 percent
  • Boomer pet owners: 17 percent
  • Silent generation pet owners: 2 percent

Millennials are the largest single segment and the highest-spending per pet.

Key growth drivers

  1. Pet humanization: pets treated as family members, budgets treated accordingly
  2. Remote work: more time at home with pets drives interaction and gifting
  3. Millennial household formation: delayed childbearing means pets as first "children"
  4. Smart home adoption: natural extension from smart home to smart pet
  5. Urbanization: apartment living favors compact smart solutions over large equipment
  6. Veterinary cost inflation: preventive tools and monitoring become financially attractive

Key headwinds

  • Economic pressure: inflation reduces discretionary spending in some segments
  • Pet adoption plateau: post-pandemic adoption boom normalized in 2024
  • Regulatory burden: increasing compliance costs in EU
  • Chinese manufacturing disruption: tariffs, shipping volatility, political tension

Frequently asked questions

Are these statistics current as of 2026?

These are compiled from 2024 APPA, FEDIAF and Euromonitor reports with 2025 updates where available. 2026 specific projections are extrapolations. Always reference the original sources in pitches.

Where can I find the original sources?

APPA.org (US), FEDIAF.org (EU), Statista.com, Euromonitor.com, Grand View Research. Most charge fees for full reports, but summaries are often free.
