How to Calculate Your True Cost When Importing Pet Products

First-time importers routinely undercount their landed cost by 30 to 50 percent and then wonder why their brand is unprofitable. The FOB price is just one line item in the total cost of getting a Chinese-manufactured pet product onto an Amazon shelf and into a customer's hands. Missing the other lines leads to pricing mistakes that kill the brand in year one. This article is the complete landed cost calculation for pet products imported from China to the US and EU in 2026, written from Hefei, China, by Eviehome (Hefei Ecologie Vie Home Technology Co., Ltd.).

The 15 cost lines of an imported pet product

A complete landed cost calculation includes these lines. Skip one and you are underestimating your real cost.

1. FOB unit cost

The price the factory charges you at the Chinese port, before shipping. This is the headline number in quotes. Example: USD 25 for a smart pet feeder.

2. Factory moulds and tooling amortization

One-time mould or tooling fees (USD 3 000 to 30 000) divided by the expected production volume. Often forgotten. Example: USD 10 000 mould over 5 000 units = USD 2 per unit.

3. Samples and development costs

Pre-production samples, design iterations, engineering time. USD 500 to 5 000 per project, amortized over first production. Example: USD 2 000 / 1 000 units = USD 2 per unit.

4. Inland freight China

Trucking from the factory to the port of departure. USD 50 to 200 per pallet. Example: USD 100 / 40 units per pallet = USD 2.50 per unit.

5. Export customs and documentation

Chinese export paperwork, commercial invoice, packing list, CO (certificate of origin). USD 50 to 200 per shipment.

6. Ocean freight

Sea container freight from Shanghai/Shenzhen/Ningbo to US/EU port. Heavily variable. In 2024 to 2025, rates dropped to USD 2 500 to 4 500 per 40-foot container. Example: USD 3 500 / 500 units per container = USD 7 per unit.

7. Ocean insurance

0.2 to 0.5 percent of cargo value. Small but worth it. Example: USD 10 000 cargo value x 0.3 percent = USD 30.

8. Destination port charges

Terminal handling, unloading, dock fees at the destination port. USD 300 to 800 per container. Example: USD 500 / 500 units = USD 1 per unit.

9. Customs duties and tariffs

Varies by product HS code and country of origin:

  • USA: pet products typically 0 to 6 percent base duty + potential Section 301 tariffs on Chinese goods (7.5 to 25 percent on various HS codes).
  • EU: pet products typically 0 to 4 percent duty + VAT (17 to 25 percent depending on country).
  • UK: similar to EU but with UK-specific VAT and post-Brexit adjustments.

For a Chinese-origin smart pet feeder imported to the US: 3 percent base + 25 percent Section 301 = 28 percent on the declared value. This can be the biggest cost item.

10. Import customs clearance fees

Customs broker fees, ISF filing, CBP processing. USD 150 to 400 per shipment.

11. Inland freight destination

Trucking from destination port to your warehouse or Amazon FBA. USD 200 to 800 per pallet.

12. Warehouse handling and storage

If you use a 3PL or Amazon FBA, there are receiving, handling, and storage fees. Amazon FBA fulfillment fee ranges from USD 4 to 15 per unit depending on size and weight.

13. Amazon referral fee

15 percent of the sale price for most pet categories. Example: USD 79 retail x 15 percent = USD 12.

14. Returns and defects

Plan for 3 to 8 percent return rate depending on product category. Cost of returns includes lost product, return shipping, customer service. Example: 5 percent return rate on USD 79 = USD 4 per unit allocation.

15. Product liability insurance

Annual policy amortized over unit volume. USD 500 to 3 000 per year for a USD 1M pet brand. Example: USD 1 500 / 3 000 units = USD 0.50 per unit.

A USD 25 FOB feeder sold at USD 79 retail yields USD 7.30 gross profit per unit (9 percent). That is not enough to cover marketing and overhead.

For this product to be profitable at USD 79 retail, the factory cost needs to be under USD 18 FOB, OR the retail price needs to be USD 99+.

Marketing and overhead (separate from landed cost)

Landed cost is just the cost to get the product into the buyer's hands. On top, you need:

  • Amazon PPC: target 15 to 25 percent ACOS on a launched product, 30 to 50 percent during launch.
  • Content marketing and SEO: USD 500 to 5 000 per month for a small brand.
  • Influencer and PR: highly variable.
  • Email marketing: USD 30 to 200 per month in software.
  • Business overhead: legal, accounting, software, office space.

A healthy brand operates at 30 to 45 percent gross margin AFTER all landed costs, to absorb marketing and overhead while still being profitable.

Pricing rule of thumb

For a pet product imported from China to the US:

  • Retail price should be 3.5 to 5x the FOB price, depending on category and tariffs.
  • A USD 25 FOB product should retail USD 87 to 125 for healthy margins.
  • A USD 15 FOB product should retail USD 52 to 75.
  • A USD 50 FOB product should retail USD 175 to 250.

If your target retail does not match this range, either renegotiate FOB, increase retail, or reconsider the category.

Common miscalculations

  • Using FOB as "total cost": ignores 50 percent of real costs.
  • Forgetting Section 301 tariffs: 25 percent on many Chinese HS codes for US import. Huge impact.
  • Ignoring Amazon FBA fees: USD 5 to 15 per unit depending on size.
  • No return allocation: returns happen. Budget for them upfront.
  • No PPC in the margin: Amazon visibility costs money. Plan for it.

Frequently asked questions

Where do I find current Chinese import tariffs?

US Harmonized Tariff Schedule (USITC), EU TARIC database. Your customs broker can help classify your HS code and calculate exact duties.

Should I ask the factory for DDP pricing to simplify?

DDP includes FOB + shipping + duties into a single quoted price. Simpler but less transparent. Use DDP to simplify first imports but verify the line items to understand the real cost structure.
